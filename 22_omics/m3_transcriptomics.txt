DNA methylation is long-term
Bisulfite sequencing: studies DNA methylation (silencing)
ATAC-seq: studies how open the chromatin is
Gene ontology: DB with a classification of genes with related functionality
eQTL: gene difference that influences how a gene is transcribed

Micro-arrays: yes/no check (needs knowledge about the sequence). Not sequencing. Cheap
Bulk: which RNA sequences are present and in which amount
    Can be used to discover new transcripts
Single-cell: like bulk, but every cell is tagged
    Single-nuclei: like single cell, but pick just nucleus. For very complex cells
    Expensive and more difficult to analyze
Spatial RNA-seq: adds spatial information
    But it looses resolution: every point contains more than one cell

PCA for low-dimensionality (~500)
    Separate by principal components
    Each PC may be assigned a 'label' (e.g. what is the biological function?)
    Distance between points is relevant
    Explained variance tells about how good the PC is
UMAP/tSNE for high-dimensionality
    Non-linear
    Distances are not relevant, axis are meaningless
    Just for visualization, "close" points are similar, nothing more
Clustering is performed independently of dimensionality reduction
    When the clustering algorithm yields results consistent with UMAP, that's a good sign
    "Separation" depends on context: if we pick neurons vs. astrocytes, we can't see differences between astrocytes. But we can see them if we pick astrocytes alone
