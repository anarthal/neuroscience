WGS = Whole Genome Sequencing
Single-nucleotide variation: a deviance from the reference genome in one nucleotide
SNPs: a single-nucleotide variation present in at least 1% of the population (i.e. relatively common)
    Usually does not give us a strong phenotype
Copy number variation: != numbers of copies of the same gene
Array-based comparative genomic hybridization: introduce probes with specific sequences to verify if they match
SNP arrays
    They also allow to know if there are deletions or duplications (studying intensity)
    In normal heterozygosis, every progenitor may have a != allele (or not) => for the same chromosome, you will be heterozygotic for some SNPs, homo for others
