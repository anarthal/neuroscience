Main feature of lipids: solved in non-polar solvents
Less diversity than metabolites, but still wide range
Types
    Triglycerides/fatty acids
    Phospholipids
    Sterols (e.g. cholesterol)
Partition coefficient = concentration_organic/concentration_aqueous
    LogP = log_10 (Partition coefficient)
    LogP > 0 => hydrophobic
Extraction
    Single-phase: mix your sample with an inmiscible solvent (e.g. isopropanol), discard the other because lipids in isopropanol
    2-phase: chloroform (lipids will be here) and methanol (other molecules)
        But chloroform goes to the bottom, so you need to cross the methanol => contaminants
    3-phase: also by hydrophobicity
Separation: for a clearer signal in MS
    Usually chromatography
    Retention time is useful, but not portable within != instruments
    Normal-phase chromatography: polar stationary phase, non-polar mobile phase
        The more polar, the longer they stay
    Reverse-phase chromatography: non-polar stationary phase, polar mobile phase
    HILIC: to separate lipids that are polar
        Polar stationary phase, polar moving phase
    IC: stationary phase ion with one charge, mobile the oppòsite => separate charged molecules
    IMS: separate based on shape, size, charge. Based on interaction with a neutral gas (e.g. He)
        Can separate isomers
        Drift time: similar to retention time
    Still overlap. You might combine methods but only when searching for known metabolites
        2D liquid chromatography
MS: similar techniques as in proteomics
    MS has problems with isomeric structures, couple with IMS for this
In-source loss of water: some ions might loose a water molecule during MS
