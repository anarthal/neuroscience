C = O => carbonyl
C = O + R, R' => ketone
C = O + R, H => aldehyde
glucose, manose, fructose have the same chemical formula (isomers)
structural isomers: type of bindings are != (e.g. glucose has an aldehyde, fructose has a ketone)
stereoisomers: type of bindings are ==, but some functional groups (e.g. alcohols are flipped)
    number of stereoisomers: 2^n, with n = number of chiral carbons
    chiral carbon if all of its 4 bindings are connected to != groups (e.g. glucose n = 4)
if OH position in carbon number 5 is on the right, D-, otherwise L-
intermediates hemi-ketals no
Fischer projection: straight line
anomeric carbon: the one that has the functional group
pyranose ring: ring with 5 C and an O
alpha: H in the anomeric carbon upwards, beta downwards
    or if carbon 6 trans OH, alpha
most of the sugars in aqueous solution is in ring form
furanose: pentagon with an O in one node
COOH tends to have negativity
glycosidic bond: OH in the anomeric carbon reacts with something else
N-glycosidic bond: glycosidic bond to a N
non-reducing sugar: all of their anomeric carbons are forming bonds
storage polysaccharides not soluble in water => no change to osmotic pressure
branches 1-6
amylases digest glycogen and work with alpha 1-4, and not beta 1-4 => we don't digest cellulose
