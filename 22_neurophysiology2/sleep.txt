Electroencephalogram waves: higher frequency means higher neuronal activity
    Delta waves (1-4Hz): deep sleep
    Theta waves (4-8Hz): start of sleep
    Alpha waves (8-12Hz): awake state, relaxed
    Beta waves (12-30Hz): awake state, alert
    Gamma waves (30-100Hz): awake start, high-level cognitive functioning
Sleep phases
    Non-rapid eye movement (NREM)
        N1: transition between wakefulness and sleep. Alpha, theta waves
        N2: light sleep. Theta waves with sleep spindles and K-complexes
        N3: moderate sleep. Delta waves
        N4: deep sleep. Delta waves
    Rapid eye movement (REM): vivid dreams, high cerebral activity. EEG as if awake
    Wakefulness levels: awake > REM > N1 > N2 > N3 > N4
    Phases repeated in periods of 90min: N1 => N2 => N3 => N4 => REM => N1...
SLEEP SPINDLE: burst of neuronal activity during N2
K-COMPLEX: high amplitude peak of neuronal activity during N2, to prevent awakening upon stimulus
