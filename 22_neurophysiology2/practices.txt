Background
    PATELLA: kneecap bone
    PATELLAR TENDON/PATELLAR LIGAMENT: distal part of the quadriceps tendon. Goes over the patella, then to the tibia (really a ligament)
    CALCANEAL TENDON: Achilles tendon
    CALCANEUS: heel's bone
    GASTROCNEMIUS: muscle from the femur to the calcaneus (calf - gemelo)
    SOLEUS: muscle deep to the gastrocnemius (calf)
MOTOR UNIT: lower motor neuron + all the nerve fibers innervated by it
Electrocardiogram
    P wave: initial auricle depolarization
    QRS complex: second peak due to ventricle depolarization. Down, up, down
    T wave: final peak, due to ventricle repolarization
    CILIOSPINAL CENTER/BUDGE'S CENTER: cluster of pre-ganglionic sympathetic neurons in lateral gray horn. Involved in pupil dilation
ODDBALL PARADIGM: test where an individual is presented with high-frequency standard stimuli, and low-frequency target stimuli (e.g. 'identify the sound that is different')
EVENT-RELATED POTENTIAL (ERP): peak in an EEG in response to an event
P300: event-related potential that appears in oddball paradigm tests when the target stimuli appears
    Approx. 300ms after the stimulus
PATELLAR REFLEX: when patellar tendon is hit, quadriceps contracts
JENDRASSIK MANEUVER: procedure to distract the individual while performing the patellar reflex. Increases reflex amplitude
CALCANEAL REFLEX: when calcaneal tendon is hit, soleus and gastrocnemius contract
PLANTAR REFLEX/BABINSKI REFLEX: when the foot plant is stroked from heel to toes, toes should flex towards the plant
    If they spread or flex in the opposite direction, possible upper motor neuron damage
CILIOSPINAL REFLEX: when pain is generated in the back of the neck, the pupil dilates
    Pain => hypothalamus => ciliospinal center => cervical ganglion => pupil dilator
