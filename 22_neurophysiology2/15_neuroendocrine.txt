The body might upregulate receptors for an hormone if it's downregulated (and vice-versa), to compensate
Hormone therapy: block hormone receptors that promote the growth of certain tumors (e.g. breast)
Agonist: for a certain receptor specialized for a certain molecule, another molecule that can interact with the receptor
    Agonist for testosterone: can interact with the same receptors
    Partial agonist: produce partial response
        May be because it has the same affinity but causes less response (competes with the original molecule)
    Inverse agonist: causes the inverse response than the natural ligand
    Antagonist: blocks the receptor and does nothing
    Competitive antagonist/reversible antagonist: competes with the agonist for the receptors
        Proportion matters - increasing the agonist compensates the antagonist
    Noncompetitive antagonist/irreversible antagonist: bind to the receptor covalently (not necessarily to the binding site) and prevent the agonist from interacting with the receptor
Circulating hormones: they circulate in blood (endocrine). Vs. local hormones (paracrine and autocrine)
Lipid-soluble: they come from lipids
    Effective when uptaken by mouth because they are not broken by digestion and can cross the intestinal barrier
    They can cross the cell membrane and affect the inside (e.g. go into nucleus and change gene expression)
Water-soluble: they can't be taken by mouth
    They can't cross the cell membrane => Cell signalling cascade with plasma membrane receptor (e.g. GPCR)
    Permissive hormone (with respect to other): one that causes synergestic effect with the other
        Mechanism: promote more receptors for the other, 2nd messenger cascade amplification
    Synergestic effect: the effect of the two hormones together is greater than the sum of the effects of the individual effects
    Antagonistic effect: the effect of the two hormones together is smaller than the sum of the effects of the individual effects
In the hypothalamus/adenohypophysis
    Hypothalamus releases hormone-releasing-hormone to portal system => increase hormone production
    Negative feedback: higher hormone concentration prevents further liberation
    Some hormones also have corresponding hormone-inhibiting-hormones. Inverse effect to hormone-releasing-hormone
