EPICRITIC SYSTEM: part of the somatosensory system with high discriminative ability (fine touch, pressure)
PROTOPATHIC SYSTEM: part of the somatosensory system with low discriminative ability (pain, temperature)
PIEZO RECEPTORS: family of cation channels, non-selective, mechanically gated
TRANSIENT RECEPTOR POTENTIAL RECEPTOR (TRP): family of cation channels, involved in many sensory modalities
MYOTATIC REFLEX: reflex that contracts muscle when stretched rapidly, to prevent damage
INVERSE MYOTATIC REFLEX: reflex that relaxes the muscle when its tension is too high, to prevent damage
Receptor adaptation speed
    PHASIC RECEPTOR: responds to stimulus variations (fast adaptation). Detects "when" (temporal component of the stimulus)
    TONIC RECEPTOR: responds to sustained stimulus with a sustained response (slow adaptation). Detects "where" (spatial component of the stimulus)
Touch and pressure related receptors
    HAIR FOLLICLE RECEPTOR: surrounds hair follicles and responds to hair movement. Fine touch
        Non-encapsulated
        May have fast or slow adaptation
        Small field receptor size
    FREE NERVE ENDING: receptor in the superficial layer of the skin. Itch, tickle
        Non-encapsulated
        Slow adaptation
        Big field receptor size
    MEISSNER CORPUSCLE: receptor in the dermis of glabra skin, for discriminative touch
        Encapsulated
        Formed by several layers of epithelial cells surrounded by a nerve fiber
        Fast adaptation
        Small field receptor size
    MERKEL CELL: receptor in the dermis of skin, for texture detection
        Non-encapsulated
        Formed by a single epithelial cell associated to a nerve fiber
        Slow adaptation
        Small field receptor size
    PACINI CORPUSCLE: receptor in the deep dermis and subcutaneous tissue, for high frequency vibration
        Onion-like layers of epithelium surrounding a nerve fiber
        Fast adaptation
        Big field receptor size
    RUFFINI ENDING: receptor in the deep dermis, joints, tendons and ligaments, to detect stretch
        Encapsulated
        Slow adaptation
        Big field receptor size
Proprioception related receptors
    MUSCLE SPINDLE: receptor within the muscle that detects muscle stretch (when the muscle becomes longer)
        Placed in the center of the muscle
        Composed of specialized muscle fibers and a nerve fiber
        Slow adaptation rate
        Triggers the myotatic reflex
    GOLGI TENDON ORGAN: receptor within the tendon that detects muscle tension (when the muscle becomes shorter)
        Composed of nerve fibers intertwined with the tendon's collagen fibers
        Triggers the inverse myotatic reflex
    JOINT KINESTHETIC RECEPTOR: receptors that transmit joint position, movement and pressure
        Composed of free nerve endings, Pacini corpuscles and Ruffini endings
