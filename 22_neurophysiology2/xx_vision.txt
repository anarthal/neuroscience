Retinal detachment: neural retina detaches from pigmented retina
Pigmented retina transforms vitamin A => 11-cis-retinal => transported to neural retina and converted into 11-trans-retinal => transported back and recycled
Rods are more sensitive to vitamin A deficiency
We have much more rods than cones
Deuteranopia: color blindness
Photopigments: in the plasma membranes of cones, and in the membrane of disks (rods). Transmembrane proteins. Change config on light absorption
    Rhodopsin (rods), red/blue/green opsin (cones)
Dark: cytosolic cGMP high, Na+ channels open, glutamate released
Light: 11-trans-retinal activates opsin => activates transducin => lowers cGMP concentration => Na+ channels close, no glutamate released
No action potentials in photoreceptors - only de/hyper polarization
Receptive field for a bipolar cell encompasses several photoreceptors
    The edge/center discrimination is due to lateral inhibition
    ON and OFF bipolar cells receive the same stimulus on light, but react differently (!= neurotransmitter receptors)
multiple rows => 1 bipolar => 1 ganglion
1 cone => 1 bipolar => 1 ganglion
bipolar cells don't trigger AP, it's just graded potentials
Ganglion cells responsive to mellanopsin: only ganglion cell type that is sensitive to light => suprachiasmatic nucleus and pupillary right reflex
