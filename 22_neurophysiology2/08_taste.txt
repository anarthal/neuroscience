Background
    TRANSIENT RECEPTOR POTENTIAL MELASTATIN (TRPM): a type of TRP channels involved in taste
EPITHELIUM SODIUM CHANNEL (ENaC): ion channel selective for Na+ ions
    Responsible for part of the salty taste
ACID-SENSING ION CHANNEL (ASIC): ligand-gated cation channel that opens in the presence of H+ (acid)
    ASIC1, ASIC2
    Responsible for sour taste
TASTE RECEPTOR TYPE 1: GPCR responsible for sweet and umami taste
TASTE RECEPTOR TYPE 2: GPCR responsible for bitter taste
Concrete receptors named TXRY
    E.g. T1R2: taste receptor type 1 member 2. Sweet detection
GPCR transduction in taste
    Ligand binds to GPCR
    Intracellular cascade
    Ca2+ is released from endoplasmic reticulum
    Ca2+ opens TRPM channels
    Na+ enters the cell => depolarization => neurotransmitter release
Detection threshold
    Bitter, sour, umami: low thresholds
    Salty and sweet: high thresholds
Neurotransmitters
    Salty and sour: serotonin
    Sweet, sour, umami: ATP
Gustatory cortex is not segregated into regions by flavor like other sensory cortices
Orbitofrontal cortex and insula cortex integrate taste and odor
