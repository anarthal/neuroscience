Utricle: acceleration in horizontal axes (X and Y axes)
Saccule: acceleration in vertical plane (Z axis)
Semicircular canals: angular movements
Macula = otolithic organs = utricle + Saccule
Cupula in semicircular canals is in the ampulla, and blocks the endolymph circulation
    The cupula bends and generates the signal
Otoliths
    Gravity provides a static signal
    Linear acceleration also triggers signal
Semicircular canals have opposite excitation directions
Type I hair cells
    Pear-shaped
    Fast signals
Type II hair cells
    Column-shaped
    Slower signals
Kinocilium: the tallest stereocillium in a cell
Tip-link: filament linking stereocillia
    Made of cadherin, mutations cause hearing loss but doesn't affect equilibrium so much
Supporting cells: generate the otoliths and gelatinous structure
Movement in the direction of Kinocilium => depolarization
    Introduce K+ in the cell => depolarization => Ca2+ enters the cell => K+ exits the cell and glutamate is released
    There is always a base signal, movements either amplify it or silence it
Pathway
    Vestibular ganglion
    Vestibular nuclei (also act as integration center)
    Less importance than other senses in the cerebral cortex, mostly reflexes
VOR: keep the eye fixed even when the head rotates
    Mediated by vestibular nuclei
Nystagmus: saccadic movements to restore what VOR has moved
Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo: otoliths get into the semicircular canals
Meniere disease: too much endolymph
Vestibulospinal reflex: prevent us from falling
    Lateral vestibulospinal: contract extensor muscles
Asymmetric tonic neck: in newborns, rotation of head causes extension of limbs
