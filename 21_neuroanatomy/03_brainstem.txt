TEGMENTAL AREAS: in pons/midbrain, the core/dorsal areas
BASILAR AREAS: in pons/midbrain, the ventral areas. In the pons, the bulbous area
PERIAQUEDUCTAL GRAY: gray matter surrounding cerebral aqueduct, in the midbrain/pons
    Pain suppression
    Cardiovascular control (e.g. heart rate)
    Emotional response
RHOMBOID FOSSA: the floor of the 4th ventricle
TECTUM OF THE MIDBRAIN: posterior part, where colliculli are
SOLITARY NUCLEUS: nucleus in medulla oblongata. Taste and general visceral afferents
    SOLITARY TRACT: tract starting in solitary nucleus
    GUSTATORY NUCLEUS/LATERAL SOLITARY NUCLEUS: part of the solitary nucleus receiving taste info
    MEDIAL SOLITARY NUCLEUS: part of the solitary nucleus receiving visceral somatosensory info
NUCLEUS AMBIGUUS: nucleus in medulla oblongata. Muscles in palate, pharynx, larynx, and some parasympathetic (SVE)
DORSAL (MOTOR) NUCLEUS OF THE VAGUS: in medulla oblongata. Parasympathetic preganglionic neurons for many organs
SALIVATORY NUCLEI: parasympathetic innervation of salivary glands, in the pons. Superior and inferior
Olive: set of nuclei in medulla oblongata/pons
    INFERIOR OLIVE: performs integration of somatosensory information from spinal cord, motor commands and other inputs, and projects to cerebellum
    SUPERIOR OLIVE: related to hearing
PONTINE NUCLEI: receive input from motor-related cerebral cortices (i.e. what's the current plan?) and other inputs. Integrates them and projects to cerebellum
RETICULOTEGMENTAL NUCLEUS: in dorsal pons
    Inputs: cerebral cortex and superior colliculus (vision)
    Outputs: projects to cerebellum
    Involved in eye movement control
CENTRAL TEGMENTAL TRACT: from lateral solitary nucleus to thalamus. Carries gustatory info. Ipsilateral
Nerve functional components: indicates the function of spinal nerve/cranial nerve fibers and neurons. Special components only in cranial nerves
    GENERAL SOMATIC AFFERENT (GSA): sensory, innervating the body wall (e.g. skin)
    GENERAL VISCERAL AFFERENT (GVA): sensory, innervating visceral structures (e.g. cardiac muscle)
    GENERAL SOMATIC EFFERENT (GSE): motor, innervating skeletal muscle
    GENERAL VISCERAL EFFERENT (GVE): motor, innervating cardiac/smooth muscle and glands
    SPECIAL SOMATIC AFFERENT (SSA): sensory, for special senses (e.g. optic nerve)
    SPECIAL VISCERAL AFFERENT (SVA): sensory, for taste
    SPECIAL VISCERAL EFFERENT (SVE): motor, innervating smooth muscle derived from branchial arches (e.g. mastication)
    Note: special somatic efferent does not exist
Cranial nerves
    HYPOGLOSSAL NERVE (XII): motor, innervates muscles of the tongue
        Nucleus: hypoglossal nucleus in medulla oblongata
    ACCESSORY NERVE (XI): motor, innervates trapezoid and sternocleidomastoid mucles
        Nucleus: in spinal cord
        Fibers from nucleus ambiguus (SVE)
    VAGUS NERVE (X): mixed
        Has two ganglia
            Inferior (further from CNS): GVA, SVA cell bodies
            Superior (closer to CNS): GSA cell bodies
        Somatic sensations from ear and other small parts: superior ganglion, spinal trigeminal tract (GSA)
        Visceral sensations from many organs (lungs, heart): inferior ganglion, solitary tract, medial solitary nucleus (GVA)
        Part of taste (SVA)
        Parasympathetic innervation of many organs: from dorsal motor vagus nucleus (GVE)
        Innervation of muscles in palate, pharynx, larynx: nucleus ambiguus (SVE)
    GLOSSOPHARYNGEAL NERVE (IX): mixed
        Has two ganglia
            Inferior (further from CNS): GVA, SVA cell bodies
            Superior (closer to CNS): GSA cell bodies
        Somatic sensations from tongue, tympani: superior ganglion, spinal trigeminal tract (GSA)
        Visceral sensations from some parts: inferior ganglion, solitary tract, medial solitary nucleus (GVA)
        Part of taste (SVA)
        Inferior salivary fibers (GVE)
        Innervation of the muscle to swallow from nucleus ambiguus (SVE)
    VESTIBULOCOCHLEAR NERVE (VIII)
        Cochlea (hearing) (SSA)
        Vestibule (equilibrium) (SSA)
        VESTIBULAR NUCLEI: 4 nuclei where vestibular branch of VIII ends. In pons
            Superior
            Medial: long, below superior
            Lateral
            Inferior: long, below lateral
        COCHLEAR NUCLEI: 2 nuclei where cochlear branch of VIII ends. Anterior, posterior.
    FACIAL NERVE (VII): mixed. Similar to glossopharyngeal
        Has only one ganglion
        Somatic sensations from part of the face (GSA)
        Part of taste (SVA)
        Lacrimo muconasal fibers: parasympathetic innervation of glands (GVE)
        Superior salivatory fibers (GVE)
        Facial expression muscles (SVE)
        Innervates stapedius
    ABDUCENS NERVE (VI): motor. Eye muscles
        Starts in abducens nucleus
    TRIGEMINAL NERVE (V): mixed
        Has 3 divisions, covering different areas in the head
        Touch, proprioception, nociception for face (GSA). This is the most prominent GSA cranial nerve
        Innervates mastication muscles and other (SVE)
        Innervates tensor tympani
        Nuclei
            Spinal trigeminal nucleus: sensory. Long, vertical, extending from pons to spinal cord
            Principal sensory: mid-pons
            Motor nucleus: next to principal sensory
            Mesencephalic nucleus: vertical, extending to midbrain
    TROCHLEAR NERVE (IV): motor. Eye muscles
        From trochlear nucleus, in midbrain
    OCULOMOTOR NERVE (III): motor. Other eye muscles
        Oculomotor nucleus (GSE)
        CILIARY NUCLEUS/EDINGER-WESTPHAL NUCLEUS: controls ciliary muscles (GVE)
    OPTIC NERVE (II): sensory (SSA)
    OLFACTORY NERVE (I): sensory (SVA)
