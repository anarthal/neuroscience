Background
    VESTIBULO-OCULAR REFLEX (VOR): maintains gaze stable when moving the head by moving the eyes in the opposite direction
General anatomy
    Medial-lateral divisions
        Vermis: the central region
        Hemispheres: everything that is not the vermis
        Paravermis: region in the hemispheres next to the vermis
    Anterior-posterior divisions
        Lobes: the main divisions, delimited by fissures. Anterior, posterior, flocculonodular
        Fissures: primary fissure, posterolateral fissure
        Lobules: each of the 10 subdivisions of the vermis and hemispheres. Designated with roman numerals from I (posterior) to X (anterior)
        UVULA: vermis lobule IX
        NODULUS: vermis lobule X. Part of the flocculonodular lobe
        FLOCCULUS: hemisphere lobule HX. Part of the flocculonodular lobe
Peduncles
    Inferior (to medulla)
        RESTIFORM BODY: the bigger part of the inferior peduncle
        JUXTARESTIFORM BODY: the smaller part of the inferior peduncle
    BRACHIUM PONTIS: middle peduncle (to pons)
    BRACHIUM CONJUNCTIVUM: superior peduncle (to midbrain)
Deep cerebellar nuclei: main cerebellar output. From medial to lateral
    Fastigial nucleus
    Interposed nuclei
        Globose nucleus
        Emboliform nucleus
    Dentate nucleus
Cerebellar circuit
    Inputs enter the cortex, as mossy fibers or climbing fibers
    Inputs also send collaterals to the linked deep nucleus. E.g. anterior spinocerebellar to fastigial
    Purkinje cell layer performs summation and sends output to the deep cerebellar nuclei
    Deep nuclei project outside the cerebellum
Vestibular system (vestibular nuclei)
    Input: vestibular, visual, somatic
    Output
        Eye movements: nuclei of oculomotor (III), trochlear (IV), abducens (VI) CN
        Head and neck movements (equilibrium): nuclei of accessory (XI) CN
        Muscle tone for equilibrium: vestibulospinal tract
        Conscious sensation of equilibrium: cerebral cortex, through thalamic ventral posterior nucleus
VESTIBULOCEREBELLUM/ARCHICEREBELLUM: balance, eye movements, modulates vestibulo-ocular reflex
    The oldest cerebellum part phylogenetically
    Corresponds to flocculonodular lobe and uvula
    Inputs: vestibular and visual (as mossy fibers)
        PRIMARY VESTIBULOCEREBELLAR FIBERS: vestibule, VIII CN, juxtarestiform body, cerebellum
            Bypass vestibular nuclei
            Collateral to fastigial nucleus
        SECONDARY VESTIBULOCEREBELLAR FIBERS: vestibule, VIII CN, synapse in all vestibular nuclei, restiform body, cerebellum
    Output: vestibular nuclei, through juxtarestiform body
    Gabaergic intervestibular system: inhibitory connections between contralateral vestibular nuclei. Enhances their function
SPINOCEREBELLUM/PALEOCEREBELLUM: control of trunk and limb muscles. Posture and correction of movements in real time (e.g. walking)
    Intermediate age phylogenetically
    Vermis: axial and proximal muscles
        Linked to fastigial nucleus
        Input: anterior spinocerebellar, trigeminal (somatosensory and proprioceptive)
        Output: vestibular nuclei (posture), reticular formation, thalamus/motor cortex
    Paravermis: distal muscles
        Linked to interposed nuclei
        Input: posterior spinocerebellar
        Output: red nucleus, reticular formation, thalamus/motor cortex
