Patch clamp
    Each experiment needs a new freshly made pipette
    Try to reduce signal/noise ratio by
        faraday cage surrounding experiment
    Brain slices must be fresh (animal recently killed)
    Microscope-guided: you can't see the pipette with naked eye
    Difficult to perform
Juxtacellular recordings
    For in-vivo (e.g. anesthesized mouse)
    Works best for cells for nuclei with pacemaker activity (e.g. raphe, substantia nigra - periodically firing)
    You can inject dye into the target cell so when you kill the animal know which cell you recorded
    Dopaminergic neurons burst when activated => this may be a stress factor => vulnerability
    Different burst patterns in different neurons (e.g. lateral more bursty than medial => more vulnerable)
