RECOMBINANT PROTEIN: protein produced by genetic engineering
BLUE-WHITE SCREENING: molecular cloning technique where recombinant bacteria can be distinguished from non-recombinant by their color
Most common host is E. coli. Different strains optimized for different purposes
    Plasmid storage: mutations for easier transformation, blue-white screening
    Yield: reduced protease and RNAse activity
CODON USAGE BIAS: describes preference of an organism to express an amino-acid with a certain codon, instead of their synonyms. != in != species
    Codon optimization: write the target gene with codons friendly to the vector => better efficiency
Affinity tag: extra cDNA corresponding to an extra peptide that facilitates purifying the protein of interest
IPTG: inducer compound (activates gene expression). Higher concentration => more induction
BLASTICIDIN: antibiotic used for selecting vectors after transformation
INCLUSION BODY: cluster of misfolded protein aggregates caused by over-expression
OPTICAL DENSITY: measure of light attenuation due to absorption. log(I_after/I_before)
Growth phases: describes how cell number varies with time when they grow
    Lag phase: initial phase, slow growth
    Log phase: exponential growth
    Stationary phase: growth stalled
    Death phase: cell die after too much time
Protein induction procedure
    Wait until cells enter in log phase
    Add inducer
    Wait for an optimal amount of time
    Lyse the cells
Variables to optimize
    When should the inducer be added? Use optical density as a measurement of how many bacterial cells there are
    Higher temperature => quicker protein synthesis, but may cause inclusion bodies/toxicity due to cell machinery overwhelm
    Higher inducer concentrations => similar effect to temperature
    Time until harvest => amount of retrieved protein plateaus at a certain time. Inducing for longer doesn't yield any better result
    Soluble proteins can be induced fast. More insoluble ones require going slower to prevent inclusion bodies
PROTEIN INDUCTION TEST: experiment to check the optimal conditions for bacteria to generate proteins
STARTER CULTURE: in an induction test, the initial bacterial culture that gets split to test different conditions
