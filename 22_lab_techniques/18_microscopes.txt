Airy disk: blur in microscope due to refraction
    Sets a limit to the level of detail (resolution) that a microscope can get
    The smaller wavelength (colder colors), the smaller the airy disk
        But higher wavelength penetrate better
    Electron microscopy removes this problem
    If your volume is big (e.g. entire organs), stereomicroscopy or light sheet
    Tissue (3-10 um): widefield, confocal
        Confocal achieves more detail
    Cells and large organelles (0.2-10um)
        If you just need to see big things (e.g. nuclei): widefield
        If you need to see small organelles with precision: confocal
    Colocalization: are 2 proteins living at the same time in the same place?
        Use confocal
    Confocal uses a potent laser and kills cells
    Deconvolution: removes out-of-focus light (post-processing)
    Nano-structures (smaller than diffraction limit): super-resolution microscope, electron microscopy
Cryo-EM: the most potent microscopy technique ATM
Contrast: ability to distinguish what we want to see against the background
    Transparent live samples
        phase contrast: for the contour
        differential interference contrast: for volume
    Transparent fixed samples: TEM
    Specific protein or structure (in its original place): fluorescence (with optical microscopy)
    Opaque objects: SEM
Microscope config
    Upright: watching the sample from above. Vs. inverted
Working distance: distance from lens to sample
Lens
    Chromatic aberration: distortion because != wavelengths refract differently
        Achro: corrects this
    Spherical aberration: distortion because a planar target can't focus on a planar surface
        Fluar: corrects this
    Magnification depends on the lens but also on the eyepiece lens
    Tube length: from lens to eyepiece lens, typically infinite for variable size today
    Numerical aperture: measures the ability to capture diffracted light. Greater magnifications => greater NA
        NA = sin(alpha) * diffraction_index
        Dry objectives: limit 1 (because n=1 for air)
        Objectives using oil: limit 1.5
    Immersium medium: what media can be used with? (lmm = multi-immersion, W = water, oil = oil, gly = glycerine)
        Important because if we introduce media with n != n_glass, rays scatter
        Also coded as a color
    Coverslip thickness: standard is 0.17mm, but all of them are manufactured
        Collar may help, but objectives have an optimal width
    DIC: suitable for polarized light
