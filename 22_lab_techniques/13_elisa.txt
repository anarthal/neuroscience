ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunoabsorbent Assay): technique to quantify how much of a specific protein is present in a protein mixture
    More quantitative than WB, higher throughput, more false positives
Target protein is the antigen. A detection antibody (protein-specific) is linked to an enzyme that produces color if substrate is added
Antibodies
    Capture antibody: antigen-specific. Attaches to the well and captures the antigen
    Primary/detection antibody: antigen-specific. Attaches to another antigen in the antibody, creating a sandwich
    Secondary antibody: not antigen-specific. Attaches to the primary antibody. Linked to the enzyme, provides signal
Sandwich ELISA: allows protein quantification
    Plate: 96-well raw polystyrene plates
        Raw polystyrene is hydrophobic => allows proteins to bind to the well surface
    Coating: add the capture antibody so it binds to the well. Incubate, then wash
    Blocking: occupy any missing space in the well surface, so further steps don't bind non-specifically
        Use bovine serum albumin (BSA), milk or similar
    Add the protein mixture, incubate, wash
    Add the primary antibody, incubate, wash
    Add the secondary antibody, incubate, wash
        Example enzyme: horseradish peroxidase (reduces H2O2 and oxidizes other substances => color)
    Add the substrate and measure
Direct/indirect ELISA: used to test antibodies
    Like sandwich, but the plate is coated with antigen, instead of antibodies. No protein mixture is added
    Antibodies added as usual
